# Enlarge and restyle the Battle Pass as a centered alternating staircase

## Goal

Make the Battle Pass substantially more legible and visually rewarding. Its progression rail must sit on the horizontal center of the screen, while the 100 milestone reward cards alternate left/right at successive rail nodes to form a vertical staircase. Enlarge the milestone and progress-summary presentation and amplify the existing neon game-themed effects without changing any Battle Pass behavior.

## Repository facts

- Battle Pass markup lives in `public/index.html`; the navigation header is `.screen-header`, and the progress summary contains the progress/points, reset date, target/keep-playing/completed copy, percentage, and `.battle-pass-progress-bar`.
- `GameView.renderBattlePass` in `public/js/view.js` dynamically creates all 100 milestone buttons, rail nodes, and the current two-column `battle-pass-scroll-content` (`.battle-pass-rail` beside `.battle-pass-cards`). It already exposes stable `data-battle-pass-*` hooks and uses milestone state classes (`--locked`, `--available`, `--claimed`, `--current`) plus node state classes.
- Battle Pass presentation styles, its ambient X/O effects, rail glow/fill, target halo, token float, entry stagger, localized claim particle burst, and `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback are in `public/css/styles.css` under the scoped `.game .battle-pass-*` selectors.
- Rules and rewards are in `public/js/battle-pass.js`; existing behavior has 100 monthly milestones, point requirements equal to the milestone number, and 1/5/10-gold rewards. Tests are in `tests/battle-pass.test.js`.

## Required implementation

### 1. Centered alternating milestone path

- Replace the visual arrangement of a left-side rail plus one card column with a row-aligned progression layout: the vertical rail is exactly at the horizontal center of the Battle Pass list/screen, and each rail node aligns with its corresponding milestone card.
- Place milestone 1 on the left of the centered rail, milestone 2 on the right, and alternate sides for every following milestone through 100. The result must clearly read as a connected ascending stair-step path while scrolling vertically.
- Add a short horizontal glowing connector from every node to that row's card. Connectors must alternate direction with the cards and carry an appropriate locked/reached/target/completed state treatment consistent with the rail.
- Update the generated DOM/layout as necessary to preserve one-to-one row alignment between cards, nodes, and connectors. Keep the existing `data-battle-pass-milestone`, `data-battle-pass-rail-node`, target, and state hooks functional so targeting, VFX, claiming, and tests continue to work.
- Keep the entire two-sided staircase usable on all supported viewport widths. Do **not** fall back to the current mobile one-column/left-rail arrangement, and do not introduce horizontal clipping or horizontal scrolling that hides reward controls. Cards may use the largest responsive width available on their half of the layout; literal 2× width is intentionally subordinate to this fit requirement.

### 2. Scale up milestones

- Increase the milestone presentation by 100% from the current design wherever layout permits: double card height, padding/spacing, level and requirement text, reward/action text, coin token, rail/node details, and associated visual spacing so cards are visibly much larger and easier to read.
- Use responsive size constraints for card width so a complete card remains visible on each side of the centered rail at narrow widths. Maintain the enlarged height, typography, token, nodes, and gaps responsively rather than reverting to a smaller mobile design.
- Preserve the current card information and states: milestone number, point requirement, gold reward, action copy, locked/available/claimed/current behavior, disabled semantics, and accessible labels.

### 3. Scale and enrich the progress summary

- Increase the full progress summary and progress meter by at least 100% from the current presentation: points total, reset date, next-target copy, keep-playing/season-complete copy, percentage, and progress bar dimensions.
- Do not enlarge the established navigation header/title or Back control; retain it as the usable navigation frame.
- Reflow the enlarged summary responsively without obscuring it or the list. Preserve current progress-bar ARIA values/text and status behavior.

### 4. Add restrained, state-aware juiciness

- Build on the existing visual language rather than introducing unrelated artwork, sound, haptics, or a full-screen celebration. Make the existing neon X/O ambience, glowing rail/progress fill, current-node halo, available-reward float, card entry stagger, and localized claim particle burst more prominent in a restrained way that supports the enlarged staircase.
- Ensure the enhanced rail, nodes, connectors, cards, and summary make current, reached/claimable, claimed, locked, and completed progress visually distinguishable.
- Retain and extend the existing reduced-motion treatment: `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` must disable/de-escalate animated effects while preserving legibility, layout, state indication, interactions, and target scrolling.

## Behavior, accessibility, and scope constraints

- This is presentation-only. Do not change the monthly cycle/reset logic, 100-milestone count, point/reward values, claim eligibility or persistence, coin handling, navigation, target auto-scroll, focus behavior, accessibility labels, or live claim announcements.
- Keep milestone controls keyboard reachable and operable in visual progression order. Decorative rail/nodes/connectors/effects must remain hidden from assistive technology.
- Keep styling scoped to `.game` and add no runtime dependencies.
- Preserve the existing responsive screen/canvas behavior and existing non-Battle-Pass screens.

## Acceptance criteria

1. The Battle Pass list displays all 100 milestones with a vertical rail centered horizontally; milestones 1, 3, 5, etc. are on the left and 2, 4, 6, etc. are on the right, each aligned to its own rail node.
2. Every node has a short horizontal connector to its card, with the connector alternating left/right and visually participating in the appropriate progress state.
3. At desktop sizes, milestone card height, internal spacing, all card text, reward coin token, rail/node treatment, and related details are visibly 2× the previous design. At narrow sizes, cards remain wholly visible on both sides without horizontal scroll/clipping and retain the enlarged height/typography/token/node treatment rather than changing to a one-column layout.
4. Points, reset, target/keep-playing/completed copy, percentage, and progress bar are at least 2× their previous visual size; the Battle Pass header and Back control are not enlarged.
5. Existing neon ambience and progress/claim animations are noticeably richer but remain restrained; with reduced motion enabled, motion is removed while all state cues, layout, and interactions remain usable.
6. Rendering/claiming still produces 100 cards and 100 nodes; current target selection, target auto-scroll, claiming, localized claim VFX, completion copy, ARIA progress information, labels, and live announcement behavior are unchanged.
7. Update/add automated coverage as needed for the alternating milestone structure and preserve existing Battle Pass tests. `npm test` and `npm run lint` pass.

## Out of scope

- Battle Pass rule, reward, persistence, or claim-flow changes
- Sound, haptics, unrelated artwork, or a full-screen reward celebration
- Changes to navigation/header sizing or other application screens
